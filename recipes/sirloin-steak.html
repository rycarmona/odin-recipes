Sirloin Steak


How to cook a sirloin steak


Cooking times (for 2cm thick)

  • Blue: 1 min each side
  • Rare: 1½ mins per side
  • Medium rare: 2 mins per side
  • Medium: About 2¼ mins per side
  • Well-done: About 4-5 mins each side

Steps

  1. (Optional) Salt your steak in advance – 2 hrs for every 1cm of thickness. This doesn't draw out the moisture, it gives the steak time to absorb the salt and become more evenly seasoned throughout.
  2. Bring the meat out of the fridge up to one hour before cooking so that it gets closer to room temperature.
  3. Season with pepper (and salt if ot previosuly) just before cooking.
  4. Heat a heavy-based frying pan until very hot but not smoking.
  5. Drizzle some (flavourless) oil into the pan and leave for a moment.
  6. Add the steak, a knob of butter, some garlic and robust herbs, if you want.
  7. Sear evenly on each side for our recommended time, turning every minute for the best caramelised crust.
  8. When you're happy, finish the steak off by rendering the fatty edge, holding the steak on its side between tongs for a couple of minutes. The fat will melt and add flavour.
  9. Leave to rest on a board or warm plate for about 5 mins.
  10. Serve the steak whole or carved into slices with the resting juices poured over.
  11. Enjoy :)
